About the study you are checking

Performance Medicine is an emerging field concerned with bringing health optimisation and performance enhancement together across high-demand settings: elite sport, the military, corporate and clinical environments. It is widely practised, yet it has no agreed definition, conceptual structure or governing framework. This programme is two linked papers that set out to change that.

PM1 · Scoping review
A PRISMA-ScR review mapping the literature and testing whether a coherent concept of Performance Medicine exists. Its finding: an active field of practice, but without an agreed conceptual architecture.
PM2 · Framework synthesis
Builds the Performance Medicine Integrative Framework and two applied tools (an operational specification and an assessment tool, PM-NEAT), presented as a developmental prototype for future testing.

To build the review, the literature was distilled into 18 key statements, the ones listed below. Each statement is linked to the papers that support it, and each paper was graded for how strongly it does so, and rated as either a main or a backing source.

Why your check matters. That first grading was done with the help of a large language model working to a fixed template, under the authors' direction, to keep the appraisal consistent. It is efficient, but it must be confirmed by an independent expert before it can be relied upon. That is your role. Your answers are recorded as part of the audit trail behind the paper.

What we would like you to do

It is straightforward. For each paper, you answer two simple questions:

  • 1Does this paper actually support the statement? Supports, partly supports, or does not support.
  • 2Is it rated at the right level? Each paper is marked as a principal, secondary or backing source. Does that look right?
Principal source
The central evidence for the statement.
Secondary source
Important supporting evidence, but not the lead paper.
Backing source
Adds weight but is not the main evidence.

Click any paper to open its full reference, a plain-English summary, and, for open-access papers, the abstract in full, with links to read the original online. There is a comment box on each; use it whenever something needs a note. You will see one statement per page — use Back and Next to move between them, and you can return to change any earlier answer at any time. Your answers save automatically on this device, so you can stop whenever you like and come back later to finish. When you are done, click Export and finish and send the file back to Andrew.
